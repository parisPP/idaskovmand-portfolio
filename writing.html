Selected writings
texts in Swedish marked with *

For more than a decade, I've covered subjects such as music, fashion, Jewish culture, dance and film for Swedish news press and anglophone magazines. Here are some of the pieces:

TIME AND SPACE DISPLACED, an interview with Moyra Davey about her book Index Cards.

An interview with Jean Paul Gaultier.*

For the pants issue of The Skirt Chronicles, a piece on the iconic, pantaloon-wearing dancer Valeska Gert: MAKING FACES.

An interview with Shira Haas and Deborah Feldman on the launch of Netflix series Unorthodox.

I once ghostwrote a fictionalblog for an opera.

A visit to Chanel’s haute couture ateliers.*

How to be sad on the internet?

Between 2014 and 2024, I wrote a regular music column for the Swedish newspaper Sydsvenskan about hiphop and R&B. (s)

For Stufft Magazine I did a piece about The Sopranos, a particular garlic soup and, most of all, about cooking for someone sick: THE LAST GARLIC BREATH.

A panic room-inspired piece on trying on swimwear.*

Interviews with professional prophets, about predicting the future.

I SMELL THEREFORE I AM, in which I tried tricking perfume department staff into curing my identity crisis.*

On the day of his passing, some words on Karl Lagerfeld’s persona(s).*

About sentimental materialism and the idea of decluttering as a soulful meditation. Could emptying one’s home not also just be a justification to start consuming from scratch? CLEANING OUT THE CLOSET. *

For the Princess Issue of I Love You Magazine, I explored the colour pink by dressing exclusively in it for a week.

Through circumstances the senior knitting group “Kaffeslabberas” joined in a collaboration with Danish artists, such as Henrik Vibskov. We talked about being in your 80s, knitting the ideas of contemporary graffiti artists and fashion designers, but more than anything else it was a conversation about death. *

My very first Paris commission was covering football player Zlatan Ibrahimovic joining Paris Saint Germain. *
